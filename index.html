Real-World Curriculum

Our curriculum, based on real-world solutions and experiences, is taught by leading scholars in the field, and you'll gain practical experience in areas such as supply chain strategy and analytics, logistics, analytical and stochastic decision modeling using Excel, IT strategy and management, and enterprise data management.

Creating tomorrow's business leaders.

The ODT Department prepares students for exciting careers on the forefront of supply chain management, operations management, business analytics, and information systems.

Undergraduate students can choose from majors in:

  • Supply Chain Management
  • Operations Management
  • Information Systems

or a co-major in:

  • Business Analytics
  • Digital Technology Management

Our theory and application courses provide the fundamental knowledge you'll need to hone your business skills. The Operations and Decision Technologies curriculum builds on such foundational courses as:

K303 Technology & Business Analysis

+

K303 evolves with changes in technology and industry demands. Today's businesses know data analytics can improve business processes. Our students learn to prepare and analyze data, and develop spreadsheet models that lead to actionable insights. The course covers Excel modeling, data visualization, and analytical decision-making frameworks.

The Integrated Core (I-Core)

+

The Integrated Core is a distinctive feature of the Kelley undergraduate experience. Students work in cross-functional teams to solve complex business problems, integrating knowledge from operations, finance, marketing, and strategy. This immersive experience prepares students for the collaborative nature of today's business environment.

K304 Supply Chain Management Fundamentals

+

This course introduces students to the principles of supply chain management, including procurement, logistics, inventory management, and demand forecasting. Students gain hands-on experience with industry-standard tools and methodologies.

K353 Business Analytics & Modeling

+

K353 focuses on quantitative modeling techniques including optimization, simulation, and predictive analytics. Students learn to use data-driven methods to make better business decisions across various functional areas.

Careers

A concentration in the ODT majors prepares you for success in a number of exciting careers in emerging and in-demand fields at corporations such as supply chain planning, global procurement analyst, purchasing and inventory manager, material planning analyst, data analyst, IT analyst, data analytics — insights and analytics, digital analytics, and systems and analytics as well as in consulting positions.
